# Local Lead Generation MVP

## Core Idea

Build simple local SEO websites that rank for service + city searches, capture real customer demand, and turn that demand into leads that can be sold, forwarded, or rented to local businesses.

Example:

- `nettoyage-canape-cergy.fr`
- `plombier-pontoise.fr`
- `serrurier-osny.fr`
- `climatisation-argenteuil.fr`

The website is not a portfolio site or a brand showcase. It is a conversion-focused local SEO asset.

Its job is to:

- rank on Google
- generate phone calls
- generate SMS/contact requests
- generate form submissions
- prove local demand

## Market Thesis

The opportunity exists because many local service providers still have weak online execution:

- old or slow websites
- poor mobile UX
- unclear CTAs
- weak local SEO
- little structured content
- no clear lead capture system
- inconsistent business information

The business is not about building beautiful websites. The business is capturing high-intent local search demand and converting it into calls, SMS messages, and form submissions.

A simple static website can be competitive when it is faster, clearer, more mobile-friendly, and more locally relevant than existing local business websites.

The difficult part is not building the first page. The difficult part is earning local visibility through indexing, relevant content, Google Business consistency, citations, and continuous measurement.

## First MVP

The first test site is:

`nettoyage-canape-cergy.fr`

Target keyword:

`nettoyage canapé Cergy`

Why this niche:

- clear commercial intent
- local service demand
- easier competition than locksmiths or plumbers
- simple service explanation
- easy lead routing through phone, SMS, and form

## Domain Strategy

For the first MVP, an exact-match local domain is a good choice.

`nettoyage-canape-cergy.fr` clearly communicates the service, city, and search intent. This can help users understand the site immediately and may improve click-through from local search results.

The goal is not to build a large brand at this stage. The goal is to test whether Google sends local traffic and whether that traffic turns into calls, SMS messages, or form submissions.

Even though the domain is specific, the implementation should stay reusable. The site should act as a template that can later be adapted to another city or niche.

Possible future examples:

- `nettoyage-canape-pontoise.fr`
- `nettoyage-canape-argenteuil.fr`
- `plombier-cergy.fr`

If the MVP works, the model can expand through more exact-match domains, a broader brand, or an internal generator.

## Validation Goals

The MVP should prove:

1. Google indexes the site.
2. Search Console shows impressions for local cleaning queries.
3. Visitors click phone, SMS, or form CTAs.
4. At least some visitors submit real service requests.
5. The data is strong enough to approach local providers.

The goal is not immediate profit. The goal is evidence.

## Success Metrics

Track:

- indexed pages
- impressions
- clicks
- average position
- phone clicks
- SMS clicks
- form submissions
- lead quality

The first meaningful success is not a large volume of leads. Even a small number of local requests can validate that the niche has demand.

## Lead Capture

Use three lead channels:

- phone call
- SMS
- form submission

Use the same phone number everywhere:

- website
- Google Business Profile
- local directories
- future outreach

For this MVP, the phone number is:

`+33 7 56 80 43 67`

## Lead Handling

Before an artisan partner is signed:

- collect the request
- answer or follow up manually
- note the city, service, urgency, and lead quality
- use the data to approach local providers

Do not make fake promises. Use honest positioning:

- estimate request
- response subject to availability
- no online payment
- no guaranteed intervention

## Monetization Models

After validation, the site can be monetized in three ways:

1. Sell leads to a local provider.
2. Rent the website and incoming leads monthly.
3. Sell the ranked website as an asset.

Possible lead pricing depends on the niche and lead quality.

Example ranges:

- 20-50 EUR per qualified lead
- 300-1000 EUR per month for a rented local asset

## Site Requirements

The MVP site should be:

- static
- fast
- mobile-first
- easy to deploy
- easy to clone for another city or niche

Required pages:

- homepage
- thank-you page
- legal notice
- privacy policy
- sitemap
- robots.txt

Required homepage elements:

- service + city headline
- phone CTA
- SMS/contact option
- short quote form
- local service explanation
- FAQ
- local area mentions
- clear privacy/contact note

Avoid:

- fake reviews
- fake company history
- fake team photos
- fake physical address
- keyword stuffing
- mass-generated thin pages

## SEO Principles

Focus on:

- primary keyword in title and H1
- local city/service language
- semantic HTML
- structured FAQ content
- sitemap submission
- fast mobile loading
- clean internal structure
- consistent phone and business details

Do not rely on the exact-match domain alone. The domain helps, but indexing, content quality, mobile UX, and local trust matter more.

## First 90 Days

Do not constantly redesign the site.

Focus on:

- Google indexing
- Search Console impressions
- local query data
- phone/SMS clicks
- form submissions
- lead quality

Only add more pages when search data suggests a clear reason.

## Expansion

After one validated site:

- repeat the same niche in nearby cities
- test another local service niche
- build a repeatable generator only after demand is proven

The scalable platform should come after validation, not before it.
